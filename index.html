NGC 6872

NGC 6872 or the Condor Galaxy is known as a spiral galaxy and has a distance of 212 million light-years from Earth, a radius of 250,000 light-years and a diameter of 500,000 light-years. It is also 5.003 years old. The galaxy was first discovered by John Herschel on June 27, 1835. It also has a radial velocity of 4613.9 km/s. The galaxy got it's alternative name "Condor Galaxy" from the condor bird.

Messier 87

Messier 87 or M87 is known as an elliptical galaxy and has a distance of 53 million light-years from Earth, a radius of 60,000 light-years and a diameter of 120,000 light-years. It is also 13.24 billion years old. The galaxy was first discovered by Charles Messier (the person in which the galaxy got it's name from) in 1781. It also has a radial velocity of 1,256 km/s.



For the official article, click here!

IC 3583

IC 3583 is known as an irregular galaxy and has a distance of 30 million light-years from Earth. It also has an apparent magnitude/luminosity of 13.3. It was first discovered by Issac Roberts on April 29, 1892. To this day, little of the cause of it's structure is known. It has been said that the galaxy had been interacting in a gravitational sense with Messier 90, one of the spiral galaxies.
