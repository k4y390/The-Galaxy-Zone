Messier 100

Origin

On March 15, 1781, French astronomer and Charles Messier’s comet discovering buddy Pierre Méchain discovered this galaxy as two comets. When Messier checked it out, he considered it as a starless nebula as it was difficult to recognize at first due to its faint light. After a while, it ended up making Messier’s catalog on April 13, 1781.

Characteristics and Features

The galaxy has a bright core in its center that was created by a black hole that immerses matter. As a result, this galaxy has mass star formation activity. Within its arms, of its spiral, it contains smaller black holes and five supernovae. Due to their lack of brightness, three of the nebulae can’t be seen too well but they can only be seen as soon as they pass the meridian in good weather. It also contains seven supernovae which were discovered over the range of nearly 120 years.

Interesting Facts

It has a diameter of 107,000 light years.

It is 56 million light years away from Earth.

It is located within the Virgo Cluster.

Its other names are NGC 4321 and the Mirror Galaxy.
