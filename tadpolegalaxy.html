The Tadpole Galaxy

Origin

Around 100 million years ago, two small galaxies slammed into each other and began their bondage.
It is believed that both galaxies’ tidal forces resulted in its distorted shape as the Tadpole’s was
pulled out and a trail of stars and gas followed the galaxy. Through the Hubble Space
Telescope, it was observed within the range of 2001 and 2002.

Characteristics and Features

The galaxy’s one million stars were a result from immense star formation that occurred
when the smaller galaxies collided. Its stars contain dark matter and dim light. As for its long
tail, the collision between the two small galaxies resulted in a trail of tiny blue star clumps and
debris that stretched out over 280,000 light years. Although, it has been considered that the star clumps
will evolve into dwarf galaxies, which will result in the loss of the tail as they develop their shape.

Interesting Facts

Its other names include UGC 10214 and Arp 188.

It is located in the constellation Draco.

Two supernovae, SN 2007cu and SN 2008dq, have been detected in the galaxy between 2007 and 2008.
