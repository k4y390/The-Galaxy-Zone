NGC 6872

Origin

On June 27, 1835, English astronomer John Herschel discovered one of the largest galaxies ever seen. The galaxy formed from morphing with its neighbor, IC 4970 into a spiral galaxy according to experts' beliefs. But, the morphing has also led to the galaxy having two arms that gives the galaxy an interesting shape.

Characteristics and Features

NGC 6872 has a diameter of 500,000 light-years, a radius of 250,000 light-years and a distance of 212 million light-years from Earth. It has a radial velocity of 4613.9 km/s and is 5.003 years old. It contains from 500 billion to 2 trillion stars in which they form 130,000 light-years away from the galaxy’s nucleus according to a study by Eufrasio and could be less than 200 million years old. Because of its morphing with IC 4970, it may also contain a tidal dwarf galaxy located 290,000 light-years from the nucleus and at the end of the northeastern arm.

Interesting Facts

It is located within the constellation Pavo.

It got its alternative name, “Condor Galaxy” from the condor bird.

According to Machacek, it is possible that the galaxy has also interacted with NGC 6876.

It has a magnitude of 10.69.
