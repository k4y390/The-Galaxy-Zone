The Milky Way Galaxy

Origin

It’s been said that gas and dust clouds drew together with the addition of two objects forming a disk around 14 billion years ago. In 1610, today well-known Italian astronomer Galileo Galilei discovered this when the galaxy was just a band of light with stars across the sky.

Characteristics and Features

The Milky Way has a diameter of 105,700 light-years, a radius of 52,850 light-years and a distance of 28,000 light-years from Earth. Its nucleus consists of a high temperature disk surrounded black hole in which radiation can occur and gas clouds moving at a high speed can be observed. It's mostly composed of dark matter and contains about 100 to 400 billion stars.

Interesting Facts

It is located within the constellation Sagittarius.

It takes 250 million years for the galaxy to complete a full rotation.

J1912-4410 is the galaxy’s rarest star.

It can move at 1.3 million miles per hour.
