Messier 87

Origin

In 1781, French astronomer Charles Messier discovered one of the largest galaxies in
the universe. During the late 1700s, it was considered a nebula until the 20th century. In
order to be what it is, it merged with other galaxies to be considered
one of the largest galaxies ever discovered.

Characteristics and Features

Messier 87 has a diameter of 120,000 light-years, a radius of 60,000 light-years and
a distance of 53 million light-years from Earth. Almost being featureless, it has 1 trillion
stars that cause the galaxy to lose luminosity as they are located away from its
nucleus. Also, their density decreases as their distance towards the nucleus increases. It
also has a black hole that is a part of its nucleus.

Interesting Facts

It is located within the constellation Virgo.

It has an estimate of 15,000 star clusters.

It can be observed best during the month of May.

It has a magnitude of 9.6.
