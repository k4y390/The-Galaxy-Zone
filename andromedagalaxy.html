The Andromeda Galaxy

Origin

In 965 CE, Islamic-Persian astronomer al-Sūfi mentions the galaxy in the Book of the Fixed Stars. Although in 1612, German astronomer Simon Marius had rediscovered what he says resembles a candle light seen through a horn. It must have formed from two galaxies being involved in an orbit 7 to 10 billion years ago. The galaxies later merged to become the Andromeda Galaxy. The merging occurred from 1.8 to 3 billion years ago.

Characteristics and Features

The Andromeda Galaxy has a diameter of 152,000 light-years, a radius of 76,000 light-years and a distance of 2.5 million light-years. The galaxy contains a red light that can be seen faintly according to astronomer William Herschel. It has 1 trillion stars within its 2 spiral arms and is known to have a cluster of stars in its center. It also contains Messier objects including M31, M32 and M110 as well as the Caldwell objects C22, C23 and C28.

Interesting Facts

It is located within the constellation Andromeda.

There has only been one supernova observed in the galaxy.

The galaxy earned its name after the princess Andromeda who’s married to Perseus in Greek mythology.

The Andromeda Galaxy and the Milky Way are 2.5 million light-years away from each other.
