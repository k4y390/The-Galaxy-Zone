The Triangulum Galaxy

Origin

During the mid 1600’s, Italian astronomer Giovanni Battista Hodierna was believed to be the
first to discover the galaxy as a nebulae compacted cloud before it was considered a galaxy. It was later
noted that Charles Messier rediscovered the galaxy in August 1764. Then, he recorded it as Messier Object
Number 33 in his catalog of Nebulae and Star Clusters.

Characteristics and Features

At the galaxy’s core, there is a nebulae that is constantly forming stars while the prime
regions for their birth are within its arms. Stars are being formed at around 0.45 solar masses per year, which is
more compared to the Andromeda Galaxy. The newborn stars are often said to have high temperatures as they carry lots
of radiation according to STScl. By considering the galaxy’s diameter of 60,000 light years and distance of 3 million
light years from the Earth, it is considered the third largest galaxy within its local group of galaxies.

Interesting Facts

It has been noted to host a dwarf galaxy known as the Pisces Dwarf Galaxy.

The best time for its observation would be after dark with a latitude between 90 degrees and -50 degrees.

It is located within the Triangulum constellation.

Its other names are Messier 33 and NGC 598.
