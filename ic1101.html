IC 1101

Origin

On June 19, 1790, German-British astronomer William Herschel had made a giant discovery. At the moment, he had no idea that what he was observing was a new galaxy. Within the time that it was discovered, the galaxy was considered to be a nebulae. It was believed that IC 1101 originated from smaller galaxies combining with others in an elliptic form to become what it is. Unfortunately, this has led to a decrease in star formation taking place. Between the 1800s and 1900s, IC 1101 joined the Index Catalog where it got its designation.

Characteristics and Features

IC 1101 has a diameter of 4 million light years, a radius of 2 million light-years and a distance of 1.075 billion light-years from Earth. Its size angular-wise is from 1.20 by 0.60 arcminutes and currently has over 100 trillion stars. The stars in the galaxy are what give it a yellowish color and help it stay alive. The stars’ lifespans are short and when they die, the galaxy decreases in size unless it continues to collide with younger galaxies. It also contains a black hole that stretches 13,700 light-years and is 40 to 100 billion solar masses. The galaxy’s black hole is also deemed one of the largest ones ever discovered.

Interesting Facts

It is located within the constellation Virgo.

It is a part of the Abell 2029 cluster of galaxies and is the brightest member of the group.

It has a radial velocity of 0.07780 km/s.

Compared to the Milky Way (the galaxy we live in), IC 1101 has more stars by an estimated 1000 times.
